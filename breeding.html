Our Premium Rabbit Breeds

We specialize in two superior meat rabbit breeds, carefully selected
for optimal growth, meat quality, and commercial viability.

New Zealand White

The gold standard of commercial rabbit farming. Known for rapid growth, excellent feed conversion, and superior meat quality with distinctive white fur and red eyes.

  • ✓ Mature weight: 4–5.5 kg
  • ✓ Excellent mothering ability
  • ✓ Fast growth rate to market weight
  • ✓ Superior feed conversion ratio
  • ✓ Hardy and disease resistant
  • ✓ Large litter sizes (8–12 kits)
  • ✓ Premium meat quality

Californian

Distinctive white rabbits with black points on ears, nose, feet, and tail. Excellent for meat production with superior muscle-to-bone ratio and calm temperament.

  • ✓ Mature weight: 4–5 kg
  • ✓ Excellent meat-to-bone ratio
  • ✓ Calm and easy to handle
  • ✓ Good feed efficiency
  • ✓ Distinctive color pattern
  • ✓ Reliable breeding performance
  • ✓ Market-preferred carcass quality

Full Outgrower Status Criteria

Complete requirements to maintain your status in our exclusive outgrower program

1

Training Certification

Hold a certificate of attendance in rabbit farming as offered by The White Meat Company. This ensures you have the fundamental knowledge and skills for successful rabbit farming operations.

2

Commercial Scale Operations

Be a commercial rabbit farmer with a complement of 5x 12dx breeders cages and 5x 6dx weaners cages. This scale ensures consistent production capacity and commercial viability.

3

Regular Supply Commitment

Make regular deliveries of rabbit produce to The White Meat Company. Failure to make deliveries for 60+ days continuously without justifiable cause will result in disqualification from the program.

4

Professional Standards

Exercise sound cuniculture practices in your rabbitry as set out by The White Meat Company during training. Maintain high standards of animal welfare and farm management.

5

Pure Bloodline Maintenance

Maintain New Zealand White pure breeds from WMCo parent stock and not dilute with breeds from unapproved sources. This ensures consistent quality across our supply chain.

6

Breeding Best Practices

Practice no inbreeding on your farms. Maintain genetic diversity and health through proper breeding protocols as taught in our training programs.

7

System Registration

Be registered and remain active on WMCo's Rabbit Management Software (RMS). This allows us to track production, maintain quality standards, and ensure program compliance.
